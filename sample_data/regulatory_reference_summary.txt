REGULATORY REFERENCE SUMMARY (Plant Compliance Team Internal Notes)
Prepared as an internal quick-reference summary of applicable standards for a spice
processing and packaging facility. For full legal text, refer to the official FSSAI,
Spice Board of India, and Factories Act documentation maintained by the compliance team.

FSSAI (FOOD SAFETY AND STANDARDS AUTHORITY OF INDIA) — Key Points Relevant to This Plant:
- Food business operators must maintain hygienic manufacturing conditions across storage,
  processing, grinding, and packaging areas, including protection from pests, birds, and
  contaminants at all open material handling points.
- Temperature-sensitive processes such as roasting must be monitored and calibrated
  regularly to ensure consistent product safety and quality.
- Packaging lines must maintain enclosed or protected transfer points wherever product is
  exposed between processing stages.

SPICE BOARD OF INDIA — Quality Standards:
- Spice Board guidelines cover quality parameters for whole and ground spice products,
  including moisture content, foreign matter limits, and traceability of raw material
  batches from intake through to final packaging.
- FIFO (first-in-first-out) traceability is expected across raw material and packaging
  material storage to avoid aged or obsolete stock reaching production.

FACTORIES ACT 1948 — CHAPTER IV (SAFETY) — Key Points Relevant to This Plant:
- Employers must maintain effective safety provisions for hazardous processes and
  structural elements, including racking and storage systems subject to load-bearing wear.
- Confined space and hot work activities require documented precautions, supervision, and
  cross-checking against other simultaneous high-risk operations in the same or adjacent
  areas.

PLANT COMBUSTIBLE DUST SAFETY GUIDELINE (internal, based on NFPA 61 principles):
- Fine organic dust generated during spice grinding operations is a recognised combustible
  material. Feeding points and grinding lines require adequate ventilation and regular
  housekeeping to prevent dust accumulation.
- Hot work or spark-generating activity in proximity to grinding areas requires verified
  dust clearance, not a visual check alone, before work begins.

This internal summary is provided for quick reference only and does not replace the full
official regulatory text maintained by FSSAI, the Spice Board of India, and the Factories
Act, 1948.
